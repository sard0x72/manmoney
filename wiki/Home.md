# ManMoney Wiki

ManMoney is a personal finance tracker that runs as a desktop app on your own machine. There is no account to sign up for, no cloud sync, and no server. Everything you enter is stored in a single SQLite file on your device, and it never leaves it.

This wiki explains what each part of the app does and how to get the most out of it.

## What you can do with it

- Keep track of your **accounts** (cash, bank, credit, investment) and watch their balances update automatically as you record activity.
- Record **transactions** — income, expenses, and transfers between your own accounts.
- Organize spending and income with **categories**, including the defaults that ship with the app and any custom ones you add.
- Set monthly **budgets** per category and see how much you have left as the month goes on.
- Track **savings goals** with a target amount, an optional deadline, and a progress ring.
- Schedule **recurring transactions** for bills, subscriptions, and regular income so you don't have to remember them.
- Explore your money over time in **Analytics** — daily cash flow, 12-month trends, and category breakdowns.
- Get a quick read on the month from the **Dashboard**, including a financial health score.
- **Export** your data to CSV, Excel, or JSON whenever you want a backup or want to work with it elsewhere.

## Good to know up front

- **It is offline and private.** Your data is a local SQLite file. Nothing is uploaded anywhere.
- **No account needed.** Just download, install, and open it.
- **Balances are driven by transactions.** You set an opening balance when you create an account; after that, the balance changes only when you add, edit, or delete transactions.
