# Dashboard

The Dashboard is the first thing you see when the app opens. It's a one-screen summary of where you stand this month, pulling together numbers from everywhere else in the app.

## The top row

Four cards give you the headline figures:

- **Total Balance** — the sum of every account's balance.
- **Month Income** — everything earned so far this month.
- **Month Expenses** — everything spent so far this month.
- **Month Savings** — income minus expenses for the month.

## Daily cash flow

A chart of income and expenses across the current month, day by day, so you can see the rhythm of money coming in and going out.

## Financial health score

A score from 0 to 100 with a label (Needs Work, Fair, Good, Excellent) and a color to match. It's a rough, at-a-glance read on the month, weighted toward two things:

- Your **savings rate** — how much of your income you're keeping rather than spending.
- Your **budget adherence** — how well you're staying within the budgets you've set.

Because budgets feed the score, it's most meaningful once you've set some in [[Budgets]]. If you have no budgets and no income recorded yet, treat the score as a neutral starting point rather than a verdict.

## Budget used

If you've set budgets for the month, a bar shows how much of your total budget you've spent, turning red if you've gone over.

## Top spending and recent transactions

- **Top Spending** lists your biggest expense categories this month, each as a proportion of the largest. "View all" jumps to [[Analytics]].
- **Recent Transactions** shows your latest few entries. "View all" jumps to [[Transactions]].

## Accounts

A compact strip of all your accounts with their balances, color-coded. "Manage" takes you to [[Accounts]].

## Tips

- Make the Dashboard your **daily glance** — open it, scan the four cards and the health score, and you know if anything needs attention.
- A health score that won't budge is usually telling you to either record more consistently or set realistic [[Budgets]].
- If Total Balance looks wrong, the cause is almost always a transaction issue, not a Dashboard issue — check [[Transactions]].
