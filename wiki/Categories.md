# Categories

Categories are the labels you put on transactions so you can see where your money goes. "Food & Dining," "Salary," "Rent & Housing" — these are categories. Good categories make budgets and analytics meaningful.

## Income vs expense categories

Every category is either an **income** category or an **expense** category. When you record a transaction, only the matching categories show up, so you can't accidentally file a paycheck under "Groceries."

The Categories page has a tab for each type, with a count next to it.

## The defaults

The app ships with a sensible starter set so you're productive immediately:

- **Income:** Salary, Freelance, Business, Investment, Gift, Other Income.
- **Expense:** Food & Dining, Transport, Shopping, Entertainment, Rent & Housing, Utilities, Health, Education, Travel, Subscriptions, Insurance, Other Expense.

Default categories are marked *Default*. You can rename and recolor them, but you can't delete them — they're always available as a fallback.

## Creating a category

Click *New Category*, choose income or expense, give it a name, and pick a color and an icon. The form shows a live preview so you can see how it'll look in lists and charts.

## Editing and deleting

- **Editing** lets you change the name, color, and icon. (The income/expense type is fixed once created.)
- **Deleting** is only available for your own custom categories, and only if no transactions use them. If a category has transactions, the app blocks the delete so your history doesn't end up with orphaned entries. Reassign those transactions to another category first, then delete.

## Tips

- Keep the list tight. A dozen well-chosen categories you'll actually use beats fifty you won't. The defaults are a good starting point — trim what you don't need rather than piling on.
- Use **color and icon** deliberately. Consistent visuals make the Dashboard and Analytics charts readable at a glance.
- If you find yourself wanting sub-categories, consider using **tags on transactions** instead (see [[Transactions]]) — categories stay broad, tags add the detail.
- Pick a catch-all like "Other Expense" for one-off things that don't deserve their own category.
