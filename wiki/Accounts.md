# Accounts

An account represents a place your money lives — a wallet of cash, a checking account, a credit card, an investment account. Accounts are the foundation everything else builds on, because every transaction belongs to one.

## What an account holds

Each account has:

- A **name** (for example "Main Checking" or "Cash Wallet").
- A **type**: cash, bank, credit, or investment. The type only affects grouping and the icon — it doesn't change how balances are calculated.
- A **balance**, shown in the account's currency.
- A **currency**. The list includes USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, CAD, AUD, CHF, INR, BRL, MXN, and UZS (Uzbek Sum).
- A **color** used for its icon and accents around the app.

## Creating an account

Click *Add Account* and fill in the form. The **opening balance** you enter here is the only time you set a balance by hand. From that point on, the balance moves on its own as you record transactions.

Set the opening balance to what's actually in the account today. If you're starting mid-month, that's fine — you don't need to back-fill old history for the balance to be correct.

## How balances change

You don't edit a balance directly after creation. Instead:

- An **income** transaction adds to the account's balance.
- An **expense** transaction subtracts from it.
- A **transfer** subtracts from the source account and adds the same amount to the destination account.

Editing or deleting a transaction reverses its old effect and applies the new one, so balances always stay consistent with your transaction history. This is why the balance field is locked when you edit an account.

## Viewing accounts

The Accounts page groups your accounts by type and shows a subtotal for each group, plus your total net worth at the top. Hover over a card to reveal the edit and delete buttons.

## Editing and deleting

- **Edit** lets you change the name, type, currency, and color. The balance stays read-only.
- **Delete** removes the account. Transactions that referenced it are left in place, but their balance is no longer tracked against that account. Delete with care — it's cleaner to keep an account and simply stop using it than to delete one with lots of history.

## Tips

- Create one account per real-world account so your balances in the app match your statements.
- Use a dedicated cash account if you spend physical cash and want it reflected in your totals.
- For credit cards, record purchases as expenses on the card account; the negative balance shows what you owe.
