# Tips and Best Practices

The app rewards a little consistency. None of these are rules — they're habits that make your numbers trustworthy and the insights actually useful.

## Build the habit, not the perfect setup

- **Record regularly.** A few minutes every day or two beats a marathon catch-up once a month. Fresh entries are accurate entries.
- **Don't over-engineer categories.** Start with the defaults, trim what you don't use, and only add a category when you genuinely miss it. See [[Categories]].
- **Set the opening balances right once.** Get each account's starting balance correct when you create it, then let transactions do the rest. See [[Accounts]].

## Keep your balances honest

- **Use transfers for your own money.** Moving cash between your accounts is a transfer, not an expense plus an income. This keeps your income and expense totals from being inflated. See [[Transactions]].
- **Fix mistakes by editing, not deleting and re-adding.** Editing correctly reverses the old balance effect and applies the new one.
- **If a balance looks wrong, look at transactions.** The balance is always a consequence of your transaction history.

## Make the insights work for you

- **Set budgets on the categories that vary.** Food, shopping, entertainment — the flexible stuff. Fixed costs don't need a budget. See [[Budgets]].
- **Do a monthly review.** Open [[Analytics]], look at last month's Overview and Categories, and adjust this month's budgets accordingly.
- **Watch trends, not single months.** One expensive month is noise; a direction over several months is signal.
- **Use tags for detail categories can't capture.** Tag a whole trip or project across many categories, then search the tag later. See [[Transactions]].

## Stay in control of your data

- **Back up before big changes.** A JSON backup from [[Settings and Data]] takes seconds.
- **Keep a copy of the database file.** The `manmoney.db` file is your complete dataset; copying it is the most reliable backup and the way to move between machines.

## Track what matters to you

- **Keep recurring entries current.** Add bills and income as they start, end them when they stop. The monthly net impact then reflects your real baseline. See [[Recurring Transactions]].
- **Update savings goals when you actually save.** They're a deliberate tracker — bump the saved amount when you move money aside. See [[Savings Goals]].
