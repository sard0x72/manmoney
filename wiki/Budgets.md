# Budgets

A budget is a monthly spending limit for a category. Budgets turn the app from a record of the past into a tool for steering the present — you set what you intend to spend, and the app shows how you're tracking against it as the month unfolds.

## How budgets work

Each budget is tied to one **expense category** for one **month and year**. As you record expenses in that category during that month, the app adds them up and compares the total to your limit. There's one budget per category per month, so setting a budget for a category that already has one updates it rather than creating a duplicate.

Budgets apply to expenses only — income isn't budgeted.

## Setting a budget

Use the month navigation at the top to land on the month you want, then click *Add Budget*, choose a category, and enter an amount. The note in the form confirms which month and year you're setting it for.

## Reading the page

When you have budgets set, the top of the page summarizes the month:

- **Total Budgeted** — the sum of all your limits.
- **Total Spent** — what you've actually spent across budgeted categories.
- **Remaining** — what's left, or how far over you are.

Below that, each budget shows a progress bar that fills as you spend. The bar shifts color as you approach the limit — calm at first, orange as you near it, red once you're close or over. Categories that have gone over the limit are also called out in a banner at the top and carry an "over" badge.

The pie chart on the right shows how your budget is allocated across categories.

## Moving between months

The arrows beside the month name step backward and forward. Each month is independent, so you can plan ahead or review a past month without affecting anything else.

## Removing a budget

Hover over a budget and click the trash icon. Removing a budget only clears the limit — your transactions and spending history are untouched.

## Tips

- Budget the few categories that actually vary and matter (Food, Shopping, Entertainment). You don't need a budget on every category.
- Set budgets at the **start of the month** and glance at the page mid-month to course-correct while there's still time.
- If you consistently go over in a category, that's useful information — either the budget is unrealistic or the spending is worth a closer look in [[Analytics]].
- Budgets feed the **financial health score** on the [[Dashboard]], so keeping them current makes that score more meaningful.
